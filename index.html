Nelson Mandela

The man who fought a lifetime for freedom.

Here's a timeline of Mandela's life:

  • 1918 - Born in Cape province, South Africa
  • 1943 - Becomes involved in anti-colonial and anti-apartheid activism, joining the African National Congress (ANC).
  • 1956 - Alongside 155 others, Mandela is arrested and charged with treason but later acquitted.
  • 1961 - Co-founds the ANC's armed wing, Umkhonto we Sizwe (MK), advocating for armed resistance against apartheid.
  • 1962 - Leaves South Africa illegally to undergo military training and seek support for the ANC's struggle.
  • 1963 - Arrested and sentenced to life imprisonment during the Rivonia Trial for conspiring to overthrow the government.
  • 1982 - Transferred to Pollsmoor Prison after 18 years on Robben Island.
  • 1990 - Released from prison after 27 years of incarceration, becoming a global symbol of resistance against apartheid.
  • 1993 - Receives the Nobel Peace Prize, alongside South African President F.W. de Klerk, for their efforts to end apartheid peacefully.
  • 1994 - Leads the ANC to victory in South Africa's first democratic elections, becoming the country's first black President.

  • 1999 - Steps down as President, voluntarily relinquishing power after a single term in office.
  • 2001 - Diagnosed with prostate cancer but continues his activism and charitable work.
  • 2004 - Announces his retirement from public life but remains an influential figure on the world stage.
  • 2013 - Dies at the age of 95, leaving behind a legacy of forgiveness, reconciliation, and fighting for justice.
